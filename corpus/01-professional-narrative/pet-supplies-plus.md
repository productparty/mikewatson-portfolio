# Pet Supplies Plus - Senior Product Manager

**Duration:** Apr 2025 - Present (Contract)

## The Role

The role is technically part product manager, part project manager. There's no dedicated product management function at the company, so I'm filling both gaps - running strategic initiatives while bringing product discipline to how work gets planned and tracked.

## The Situation When I Arrived

The company had no IT development visibility, no metrics, no consistent methods across teams. Everyone just did work and told each other when it was done. There was no way to tell the story of how a project was going from a health perspective, and no framework for executives to make decisions or understand status in a simple way.

## What I'm Running

I'm currently managing 5 of the top 10 company initiatives from validation through delivery. All marketing-focused business drivers targeting revenue growth and customer experience improvement. Two stand out:

### 1. Customer Data Modernization (Company's #1 Strategic Priority)

This project affects 18M customers across 800 stores. It had stalled for months before I took it over in May 2025. The work required coordinating 7 cross-functional teams (6 internal, 1 vendor).

I took a stalled initiative and got it moving. Delivered the pilot successfully, and full rollout is on track for April 2026.

The challenge wasn't technical. It was organizational. Too many teams, no single owner, no clear timeline. My job was to create the structure that made progress possible: clear ownership, shared timelines, regular checkpoints, and executive alignment on what "done" looked like at each stage.

### 2. Pricing Project (Company's #3 Strategic Priority)

This one was floundering when I took it over. Originally slated for May 2026 release. I coordinated 4 departments and 1 vendor, cut through the ambiguity, and shipped it in March 2026. Two months early.

## What I Brought

Pretty traditional project management stuff - but it didn't exist there:
- Structured intake process and 2-week sprint cadence in JIRA
- Health perspective on project status
- Epic/feature/story framework and stakeholder alignment process
- Facilitation of executive conversations so they could make decisions
- Simple visibility into what's happening and what's blocked

Nothing revolutionary, but foundational. Sometimes the value is just bringing discipline to chaos.

## The Broader Context

The product market has been tough. This role was an opportunity to stay engaged while building toward what's next - including this portfolio bot.

There's no product management function here, so I'm bringing what I can while hoping to get as many of my projects live as possible during my time.

## Key Accomplishments

- Delivered customer data modernization project across 7 cross-functional teams (6 internal, 1 vendor), piloting the company's #1 strategic priority affecting 18M customers after it had stalled for months
- Shipped pricing project 2 months early (March 2026 vs. May 2026 target), coordinating 4 departments and 1 vendor
- Created structured product intake and vetting system with epic/feature/story framework that transformed how marketing initiatives move from concept to delivery
- Currently managing 5 of the top 10 company initiatives from validation through delivery

## What This Role Is Teaching Me

- Sometimes the job is bringing discipline where there was none
- Hybrid PM/project manager roles exist in companies without mature product orgs
- Even "traditional" project management adds real value when it's missing entirely
- Unsticking stalled initiatives is about organizational clarity, not technical solutions

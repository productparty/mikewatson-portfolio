Bad user experiences cause higher customer churn rates.

We are often in discussions with leadership and have to weigh the impact and benefits of a feature to decide if it should be built. The decisions are based on face value metrics that focus on whether you should invest in revenue-generating or customer experience-improving features.

Suppose you and your team prioritize the revenue-generating features only. You may find that the lack of focus on user experience will lead to feature-laden tech, which could run into problems such as system stability issues over time. These issues can add up and directly lead to a fleeing of customers you fought so hard to attain.

So how might we better support the need to improve the customer experience for the sake of future revenue generation?
